Typeflux lives in your macOS menu bar and turns your voice into text — right where your cursor is. Hold a hotkey, speak, release, and the result is typed into whatever app you're using. No mode-switching, no separate recording window, no copying and pasting.

Beyond plain dictation, Typeflux also lets you select existing text and rewrite it with a voice instruction, or ask questions and get answers in a floating panel.

## How it works

<Steps>
  <Step title="Press and hold your hotkey">
    The default hotkey activates Typeflux. A floating overlay appears showing your audio level in real time.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Speak naturally">
    Talk at a normal pace. If your provider supports streaming, you'll see a live transcription preview in the overlay.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Release to finish">
    Releasing the hotkey stops the recording and begins transcription. The result is automatically inserted at your cursor.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Text appears in your app">
    Typeflux uses macOS Accessibility APIs to type the text directly. The result is also copied to your clipboard as a fallback.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Permissions required

Typeflux requires three macOS permissions to operate. The app guides you through granting each one on first launch.

<CardGroup cols={3}>
  <Card title="Microphone" icon="microphone">
    Required to record audio when the hotkey is held.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Accessibility" icon="accessibility">
    Required to insert text and read selected text in other apps.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Speech Recognition" icon="wave-square">
    Required when using Apple Speech or Apple fallback mode.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

<Note>
  All audio processing happens locally or via your own API keys. Typeflux does not send data to any Typeflux servers.
</Note>
