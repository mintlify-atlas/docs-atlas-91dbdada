Typeflux is a macOS menu bar voice input tool built in Swift. It is designed around a single fast loop: hold a hotkey, speak naturally, release, and have the transcribed text inserted directly into whatever app you are working in.

The project targets people who want voice input to feel like a native part of desktop typing — not a separate recording app you switch to. Typeflux sits quietly in the menu bar and gets out of your way until you need it.

## How it works

The core interaction is a hold-to-talk gesture:

1. Press and hold the activation hotkey.
2. Speak. A lightweight floating overlay shows recording status and a live transcription preview.
3. Release the hotkey to finish recording.
4. Typeflux transcribes your speech and injects the result into the currently focused app. The result is also copied to the clipboard as a fallback.

A locked recording mode is also available for longer sessions where holding the key is inconvenient.

## Two main modes

**Dictation mode** is the default. Speak and receive text at your cursor position.

**Voice editing mode** activates when you have text selected before pressing the hotkey. Typeflux treats the selection as context and applies your spoken instruction to rewrite or transform it — useful for polishing, shortening, translating, or rephrasing existing content with a voice command.

## Key highlights

- **Multiple speech-to-text providers** — Apple Speech, Whisper API, OpenAI-compatible remote APIs, local WhisperKit models, Alibaba Cloud realtime ASR, Doubao realtime ASR, and Groq. The routing layer can fall back to Apple Speech automatically.
- **LLM rewriting** — Rewrite and post-processing workflows support OpenAI-compatible services and Ollama for local model serving.
- **Personas** — Configurable system prompts let you shape how the LLM rewrites your text.
- **History** — Typeflux stores session history, lets you retry previous records, and includes audio and text session review.
- **Local-first privacy** — The architecture supports fully local transcription setups. No audio needs to leave your machine if you choose a local provider.
- **Streaming transcription** — Providers that support streaming show you near-live feedback as you speak.
